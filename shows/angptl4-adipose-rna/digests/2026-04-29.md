**Subject:** ANGPTL4 & Adipose RNA — Wednesday April 29

**Bottom line:** A strong week for ANGPTL4 biology — five new papers expand its role across diabetic retinopathy, hepatocellular carcinoma, ARDS, oropharyngeal cancer, and diabetic nephropathy — but no fresh reports this week on adipose-targeted siRNA, ASO, or LNP delivery.

---

## Papers

**ANGPTL4 Induces Aberrant Lymphatic-Like Remodeling in Proliferative Diabetic Retinopathy.**
Li Z, Guan L, Mu T, Zhou H, Zong T, et al. *Diabetes* (May 2026).
[PMID 41805865](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41805865/) · [DOI](https://doi.org/10.2337/db25-0445)

The most clinically provocative paper of the week. Vitreous from PDR patients with poor anti-VEGF response showed elevated ANGPTL4 and VEGF-C — and the elevation was not normalized by anti-VEGF therapy. In diabetic mouse retinas, full-length ANGPTL4 and its C-terminal fragment drove pathologic angiogenesis plus a lymphatic-like remodeling signature (LYVE1, PROX1, VEGFR3). ANGPTL4 knockdown in oxygen-induced retinopathy reduced hypoxia, neovascularization, and leakage. Mechanistically, ANGPTL4 binds NRP1/VEGFR3 to drive lymphatic endothelial proliferation independent of the angiopoietin–Tie axis, and activates AP-1 to upregulate Cd83 in microvascular endothelium. The data position ANGPTL4 as a candidate target in anti-VEGF–refractory PDR.

**ESM1 drives cancer angiogenesis and bevacizumab resistance via trioleate synthesis.**
Chen X, Wan J, Jiang Z, Zhao Z, Tang C, et al. *Neoplasia* (March 2026).
[PMID 41864037](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41864037/) · [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.neo.2026.101298)

In hepatocellular carcinoma, ESM1 and ANGPTL4 form a positive feedback loop that stabilizes fatty acid synthase and pushes lipid metabolism toward trioleate. Trioleate then activates NF-κB/IL-17 signaling in tumor cells and induces CD99 on endothelium, fueling angiogenesis. Tumor growth in xenografts was suppressed by ESM1/ANGPTL4 knockdown and rescued by trioleate add-back. Patient samples confirmed elevated ESM1/ANGPTL4 in bevacizumab-resistant disease with poor prognosis. The work suggests an ESM1–ANGPTL4–FASN–trioleate axis as a combination-therapy node in anti-angiogenic-refractory HCC.

**The PPARβ/δ-Induced Mesenchymal Stromal Cell Secretome Has Cytoprotective Effects via ANGPTL4 in a Pre-Clinical Model of Acute Lung Inflammation.**
Tunstead C, Dunlop M, Ryan S, Volkova E, Johnston E, et al. *FASEB Journal* (April 2026).
[PMID 42007763](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/42007763/) · [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1096/fj.202504659R)

A new MSC-engineering angle. Human bone-marrow MSCs treated with a PPARβ/δ agonist secreted higher levels of ANGPTL4, and that secretome improved CALU-3 lung-epithelial repair in vitro and endothelial barrier integrity in an LPS acute-lung-injury mouse model. "Licensing" the MSCs with serum from ARDS patients further amplified the effect, and an anti-ANGPTL4 antibody confirmed ANGPTL4 was the key effector. The result reframes ANGPTL4 not just as a circulating lipid regulator, but as a paracrine repair factor that can be deliberately upregulated in cell therapies for ARDS.

**Inhibitory role of angiopoietin-like 4 for cancer progression in oropharyngeal squamous cell carcinoma.**
Mikoshiba T, Sekimizu M, Nakahara N, Saito S, Yoshihama K, et al. *Oncology Reports* (April 2026).
[PMID 42028751](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/42028751/) · [DOI](https://doi.org/10.3892/or.2026.9122)

A reminder that ANGPTL4 is highly tissue-context dependent. In a retrospective cohort of 137 OPSCC patients, low ANGPTL4 expression (<7.7%) was an independent predictor of worse overall and disease-free survival (HR 3.68 and 2.96). siRNA knockdown of ANGPTL4 in FaDu cells increased proliferation and Ki-67. GSEA implicated cell-cycle progression in low-ANGPTL4 tumors. Clinically, ANGPTL4 is a candidate prognostic biomarker in OPSCC — and a cautionary note for any program that pursues systemic ANGPTL4 inhibition without tissue selectivity.

**Impact of angiopoietin-like 4 (ANGPTL4) protein and O-linked β-N-acetylglucosamine (O-GlcNAc) in diabetic nephropathy.**
Pandey A, Badruddeen, Akhtar J, Khan MI, Ahmad M, et al. *Diabetes Research and Clinical Practice* (March 2026; review).
[PMID 41833781](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41833781/) · [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.diabres.2026.113211)

A timely synthesis tying ANGPTL4 to two of the dominant pathogenic pathways in diabetic nephropathy: lipid dysregulation through LPL inhibition, and tissue remodeling through integrin engagement. The review also covers the parallel role of O-GlcNAcylation in DN and proposes ANGPTL4 as a therapeutic and prognostic node in early renal disease. Useful background reading for anyone modeling ANGPTL4 modulation across cardiometabolic complications.

*Honorable mention:* a Scientific Reports paper this week (PMID 42045301, Yang et al.) reports that propofol anesthesia upregulates ANGPTL4 to suppress colon-cancer cell behavior in vitro — a small but novel pharmacology link if anyone is tracking peri-operative effects on ANGPTL4 expression.

## What to watch

The PDR paper is the one to track. ANGPTL4-driven lymphatic-like remodeling, NRP1/VEGFR3 binding, and persistence of the signal under anti-VEGF therapy could open a real second-line target in retinopathy — and connects mechanistically to the HCC bevacizumab-resistance story, where ANGPTL4 is again rising under VEGF blockade. The OPSCC data are the counter-weight: ANGPTL4 is protective in some epithelia, so any therapeutic program will need a tissue-selective delivery story — which loops back to the gap we saw this week, namely the absence of adipose- or tissue-tropic RNA delivery papers in the indexed literature. Worth queuing preprint servers and conference abstracts for that side of the field.
