**1. 2026 ACC/AHA/AACVPR/ABC/ACPM/ADA/AGS/APhA/ASPC/NLA/PCNA Guideline on the Management of Dyslipidemia**
Blumenthal R, Morris P, Gaudino M, et al.
*Circulation*, April 28, 2026
[PubMed PMID: 41824552](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41824552/) · [DOI: 10.1161/CIR.0000000000001423](https://doi.org/10.1161/CIR.0000000000001423)

The first major US lipid guideline since 2018, and the first to fold severe hypertriglyceridemia and elevated Lp(a) into the same management framework as LDL-C. The writing committee's evidence base spans literature from October to December 2024, but the document is structured to live alongside the new generation of agents — apoC-III- and ANGPTL3-directed therapeutics, Lp(a) silencers, and the bempedoic acid / inclisiron portfolio — by replacing the 2018 cholesterol-only scope with an explicit "dyslipidemias" framing. For the SHTG community, the practical question now is how the guideline phrases the threshold for initiating apoC-III- or ANGPTL3-directed therapy and how it handles the pancreatitis-prevention indication that was the basis of CORE-TIMI 72. Expect this to drive payer policy and clinical-trial enrollment criteria for the next 5–7 years.

**2. Olezarsen for Hypertriglyceridemia and Pancreatitis Risk — three-letter correspondence exchange**
Wang S, Fan Z (PMID 42054689) · Chen R, Lu S, Chen M (PMID 42054688) · Marston NA, Bergmark BA, Sabatine MS — Reply (PMID 42054690)
*N Engl J Med*, April 30, 2026
[Letter 1 (Wang)](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/42054689/) · [Letter 2 (Chen)](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/42054688/) · [Author Reply](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/42054690/) · [DOI: 10.1056/NEJMc2602622](https://doi.org/10.1056/NEJMc2602622)

Three letters published together in response to the CORE-TIMI 72a/72b trials of olezarsen in severe hypertriglyceridemia (Marston et al., NEJM Jan 29, 2026), which reported placebo-adjusted triglyceride reductions in the 49–72% range and an 85% relative reduction in acute pancreatitis (rate ratio 0.15, 95% CI 0.05–0.40). The two correspondent letters from independent groups in China raise the issues that typically follow a positive event-driven trial: the absolute pancreatitis event count is small (driving wide confidence intervals), the trial population was selected for triglyceride elevation rather than prior pancreatitis, and durability beyond 12 months is unknown. Marston, Bergmark, and Sabatine reply on behalf of the TIMI group. No new primary data, but the exchange is worth reading as a preview of the scrutiny olezarsen will face from FDA review committees and payers as the apoC-III silencing class expands.

---

**What to watch:** With the 2026 dyslipidemia guideline now anchoring the field, attention shifts to whether the pivotal cardiovascular outcomes trials for olezarsen (the larger Phase 3 CVOT in moderate hypertriglyceridemia) and plozasiran will be powered to convert the pancreatitis-prevention signal into a hard CV endpoint. Watch also for the next ANGPTL3 readouts — both the small-molecule and ASO programs have been telegraphing summer data drops. On the LPL side, gene-therapy programs for familial chylomicronemia syndrome remain quiet but active; any shift there will be the most important SHTG story of 2026.
