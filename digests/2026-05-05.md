### 1. Plozasiran Prevents Recurrent Pancreatitis in Adults With Very Severe Hypertriglyceridemia — Post Hoc of the Phase 3 PALISADE Study

**Loomba R, de-Madaria E, Afghani E, Singh VK, et al.**  
*Alimentary Pharmacology & Therapeutics* | May 2026  
[PMID 41923352](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41923352/) | [DOI 10.1111/apt.70623](https://doi.org/10.1111/apt.70623)

Plozasiran, an RNA interference therapy targeting hepatic apoC-III, delivered a striking 83% relative risk reduction in recurrent acute pancreatitis (HR 0.17; 95% CI 0.03–0.87; p=0.017) in patients with extreme hypertriglyceridemia (median baseline TGs >2000 mg/dL) who had already experienced at least one prior episode. Among 67 participants randomized 2:1:2:1 to plozasiran 25 mg, plozasiran 50 mg, or placebo quarterly for 12 months, plozasiran drove median TG levels below 500 mg/dL — a threshold widely associated with reduced pancreatitis risk. Hospitalization rates for abdominal pain, AP severity, and length of stay all favored the active treatment arms. This post-hoc analysis of PALISADE is now the first prospective, randomized evidence of a statistically significant reduction in recurrent AP in familial chylomicronemia syndrome, adding clinical urgency to the apoC-III inhibitor class that includes both olezarsen and plozasiran.

### 2. 2026 ACC/AHA/AACVPR/ABC/ACPM/ADA/AGS/APhA/ASPC/NLA/PCNA Guideline on the Management of Dyslipidemia

**Blumenthal RS, Morris PB, Gaudino M, Johnson HM, et al.**  
*Circulation* | April 28, 2026  
[PMID 41824552](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41824552/) | [DOI 10.1161/CIR.0000000000001423](https://doi.org/10.1161/CIR.0000000000001423)

The first major joint U.S. dyslipidemia guideline since 2018 is now published, formally replacing the prior blood cholesterol document. This comprehensive revision — co-authored by eleven professional societies — explicitly addresses hypertriglyceridemia and elevated Lp(a) as targets of evaluation and management alongside LDL-C. The literature search covered studies through December 2024, meaning it captures the olezarsen, plozasiran, and vupanorsen trial data. Clinicians managing SHTG patients should review the new TG-specific recommendations carefully; the guideline's updated framing of hypertriglyceridemia as a distinct management target — not simply a cardiovascular risk modifier — represents a meaningful conceptual shift. Full text is available in *Circulation* and is likely to anchor clinical practice for the next several years.

### 3. Chiglitazar in MASLD With Hypertriglyceridemia and Insulin Resistance: A Phase II Randomized, Double-Blind, Placebo-Controlled Study

**Sun Y, Wu C, Xin G, Zhong B, et al.**  
*Hepatology* | May 1, 2026  
[PMID 40720744](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/40720744/) | [DOI 10.1097/HEP.0000000000001475](https://doi.org/10.1097/HEP.0000000000001475)

Chiglitazar, a PPAR pan-agonist (α/γ/δ), demonstrated dose-dependent reductions in liver fat content by MRI-PDFF in 104 patients with MASLD comorbid with hypertriglyceridemia and insulin resistance over 18 weeks: −28.1% for 48 mg and −39.5% for 64 mg vs −3.2% for placebo. Beyond liver fat, chiglitazar improved ALT, AST, γ-GT, lipid parameters, insulin resistance, and metabolic syndrome indicators. Both doses were well tolerated, with adverse events rated mild-to-moderate. The intersection of MASLD and hypertriglyceridemia represents a common and clinically challenging phenotype, and this is the first prospective RCT evidence that a PPAR pan-agonist can meaningfully address both dimensions simultaneously. Phase III planning will be the key next step to watch.

### 4. ANGPTL4 Induces Aberrant Lymphatic-Like Remodeling in Proliferative Diabetic Retinopathy

**Li Z, Guan L, Mu T, Zhou H, et al.**  
*Diabetes* | May 1, 2026  
[PMID 41805865](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41805865/) | [DOI 10.2337/db25-0445](https://doi.org/10.2337/db25-0445)

This mechanistic study establishes ANGPTL4 as a driver of a previously underappreciated pathological process in proliferative diabetic retinopathy: lymphatic-like remodeling of retinal vasculature. ANGPTL4 — and specifically its C-terminal fragment — was elevated in the vitreous of anti-VEGF-resistant PDR patients and promoted neovascularization, abnormal VEGFR3 expression, and CD4+ T-cell/dendritic cell infiltration in diabetic murine retinas. Mechanistically, ANGPTL4 bound NRP1/VEGFR3 to drive lymphatic endothelial cell proliferation independently of the canonical angiopoietin-Tie pathway. ANGPTL4 knockdown in an OIR model alleviated hypoxia, neovascularization, and vascular leakage. While the retinal context differs from the lipolytic role of ANGPTL4 in triglyceride metabolism, these findings underscore the breadth of ANGPTL4 biology and suggest it may be a druggable target in diabetic eye disease.

## What to Watch

The week's most actionable item is the 2026 ACC/AHA dyslipidemia guideline — worth a close read for how the committee has handled SHTG specifically, and whether ANGPTL3, APOC3, and LPL pathways factor into their risk stratification or therapeutic algorithms. The plozasiran PALISADE post-hoc is compelling: an 83% reduction in recurrent AP in familial chylomicronemia syndrome is the kind of hard endpoint the field has been waiting for, though post-hoc status warrants the usual caution. The Nature Reviews Cardiology piece on apoC-III inhibitors from Gaudet, Larouche, and Stroes — once its abstract goes live — will likely be a useful synthesis of where olezarsen and plozasiran fit in real-world practice. And the MASLD-hypertriglyceridemia intersection addressed by the chiglitazar trial points toward an underserved population that deserves a dedicated therapeutic strategy.
