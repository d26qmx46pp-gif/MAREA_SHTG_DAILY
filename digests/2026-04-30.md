# SHTG / ANGPTL / APOC3 / LPL — Daily Digest — Thursday April 30

**The 2026 ACC/AHA/AACVPR/ABC/ACPM/ADA/AGS/APhA/ASPC/NLA/PCNA Guideline on the Management of Dyslipidemia**
Blumenthal R, Morris P, Gaudino M, Johnson H, Anderson T, Bittner V, Blankstein R, Brewer L, Cho L, de Ferranti S, Gianos E, Gluckman T, Gradney K, Isiadinso I, Lloyd-Jones D, Marrs J, Martin S, McLain K, Mehta L, Mora S, Mulugeta W, Natarajan P, Navar A, Orringer C, Polonsky T, Reynolds H, Saseen J, Shapiro M, Soffer D, Tynes S, Villavaso C, Virani S, Wilkins J.
*Circulation*, 2026 Apr 28.
[PubMed PMID: 41824552](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41824552/) · [DOI: 10.1161/CIR.0000000000001423](https://doi.org/10.1161/CIR.0000000000001423)

This is the long-awaited replacement for the 2018 AHA/ACC blood-cholesterol guideline, and — notably for this audience — the scope has been broadened from "blood cholesterol" to "dyslipidemia," with hypertriglyceridemia and elevated lipoprotein(a) explicitly named in the structure section as co-equal management targets. The methods section describes a comprehensive literature search through December 2024 across MEDLINE, EMBASE, Cochrane, and AHRQ databases, anchoring evidence appraisal to formal systematic reviews. PubMed's record exposes only the AIM/METHODS/STRUCTURE sections of the abstract; the full document — with the specific recommendations on TG thresholds, icosapent ethyl, fibrates, and the new-generation APOC3/ANGPTL3 nucleic-acid therapeutics — needs to be pulled from *Circulation* directly. Worth blocking out time today to read the dyslipidemia and TG sections in full and to flag where olezarsen, plozasiran, evinacumab, vupanorsen-successor compounds, and zodasiran sit in the recommendation hierarchy. This is the document SHTG clinicians and pipeline teams will be cited against for the next several years.

**Olezarsen for Hypertriglyceridemia and Pancreatitis Risk** (correspondence cluster — three letters and authors' reply)
Chen R, Lu S, Chen M (Fuzhou University Affiliated Provincial Hospital); Wang S, Fan Z (Dalian Medical University); reply by Marston NA, Bergmark BA, Sabatine MS (Brigham and Women's Hospital / TIMI Study Group).
*N Engl J Med* 2026; 394(17): 1762–1764, Apr 30.
[PubMed PMIDs: 42054688](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/42054688/), [42054689](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/42054689/), [42054690](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/42054690/) · [DOI: 10.1056/NEJMc2602622](https://doi.org/10.1056/NEJMc2602622)

A coordinated three-letter correspondence package responding to the Marston/Bergmark/Sabatine olezarsen-Essence/Core trial published in *NEJM* this past January (PMID 41211918), which reported a meaningful triglyceride-lowering signal and a numerically lower pancreatitis-event rate with the APOC3-targeting antisense oligonucleotide in patients with hypertriglyceridemia. PubMed does not host the letter texts (NEJM correspondence is rarely indexed with abstracts), but the page placement (1762–1764), shared DOI, and CommentOn target make clear that this is a focused methodological exchange — typical concerns in this format are subgroup heterogeneity, the role of fasting versus non-fasting TGs, on-treatment vs. intention-to-treat pancreatitis adjudication, and the generalizability of an FCS-anchored mechanism to broader SHTG. Marston, Bergmark, and Sabatine reply on behalf of the trialists. Worth reading the actual letters in *NEJM*'s correspondence section today — the technical pushback often previews how regulators and guideline committees will frame ongoing label expansion and the parallel plozasiran (siRNA-APOC3) program. For SHTG drug-development teams, this is the clearest live signal of where reviewers think the olezarsen pancreatitis data are most fragile.

Expect commentary pieces in *JACC*, *Eur Heart J*, and *Lancet Diab Endocrinol* over the next two weeks unpacking the TG sections of the guideline; full-text deep reads of both the guideline and the olezarsen letters are higher-yield right now than waiting for next week's PubMed dump.
