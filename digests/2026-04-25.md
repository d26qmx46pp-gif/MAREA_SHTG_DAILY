### 1. Plozasiran: First Approval (Drug Profile)

**Authors:** Syed YY  
**Journal:** Drugs | May 2026  
**PMID:** [41787196](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41787196/) | **DOI:** [10.1007/s40265-026-02293-2](https://doi.org/10.1007/s40265-026-02293-2)

**Summary:** This *Drugs* journal profile summarizes the clinical development milestones for plozasiran (Redemplo®), the first GalNAc-conjugated siRNA approved for FCS. Plozasiran received FDA approval on November 18, 2025, as an adjunct to diet to reduce triglycerides in adults with familial chylomicronemia syndrome — the first RNA-interference therapy approved specifically for a hypertriglyceridemia indication. The drug degrades APOC3 mRNA via RNAi, reducing hepatic and serum ApoC3 and thereby enhancing triglyceride clearance. The profile covers the Phase 1–3 development arc, dosing (25 mg and 50 mg quarterly subcutaneous injections), and the regulatory path. This is a useful reference article for anyone needing a concise single-source summary of the plozasiran development story and approval basis, though it adds no new clinical data beyond what has been published.

### 2. Plozasiran Prevents Recurrent Pancreatitis in Very Severe Hypertriglyceridemia — PALISADE Post Hoc

**Authors:** Loomba R et al.  
**Journal:** Alimentary Pharmacology & Therapeutics | May 2026  
**PMID:** [41923352](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41923352/) | **DOI:** [10.1111/apt.70623](https://doi.org/10.1111/apt.70623)

**Summary:** *(Also covered Friday April 24.)* Post-hoc analysis of PALISADE in 67 patients with extreme hypertriglyceridemia (median TG >2000 mg/dL) and prior acute pancreatitis. Plozasiran reduced recurrent AP risk by 83% (HR 0.17, p=0.017) and drove TGs below 500 mg/dL in most treated patients. First prospective RCT evidence for pancreatitis prevention in FCS.

### 3. Effect of ANGPTL3 Inhibitors on Lipid Profile — Meta-Analysis of RCTs

**Authors:** Xie S et al.  
**Journal:** European Journal of Preventive Cardiology | April 22, 2026  
**PMID:** [42019015](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/42019015/) | **DOI:** [10.1093/eurjpc/zwag230](https://doi.org/10.1093/eurjpc/zwag230)

**Summary:** *(Also covered Friday April 24.)* Nine RCTs, n=1,254. ANGPTL3 inhibition: TG -47%, LDL-C -22%, remnant cholesterol -73%, VLDL-C -41%. siRNAs show stronger VLDL lowering vs. evinacumab; evinacumab drives greater LDL-C reduction. No CV outcomes data yet.

### 5. Novel TG-Lowering Therapies in FCS — Updated Review

**Authors:** Joubert S et al.  
**Journal:** American Journal of Cardiovascular Drugs | May 2026  
**PMID:** [41239175](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41239175/) | **DOI:** [10.1007/s40256-025-00774-5](https://doi.org/10.1007/s40256-025-00774-5)

**Summary:** *(Also covered Friday April 24.)* Systematic review of Phase 3 trials for volanesorsen, olezarsen, and plozasiran in FCS. TG reductions: volanesorsen 73–77%, plozasiran 78–80%, olezarsen 22–44 percentage points vs. placebo. All three reduced acute pancreatitis events vs. placebo. A useful side-by-side comparison of the three approved/approvable APOC3 inhibitors.
