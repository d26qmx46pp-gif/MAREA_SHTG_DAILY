Subject: SHTG / ANGPTL / APOC3 / LPL — Daily Digest — Saturday May 2

**Bottom line:** A quiet week for new mechanistic and trial data, but two notable items: the 2026 ACC/AHA dyslipidemia guideline drops in Circulation (now formally folding hypertriglyceridemia into the same document as cholesterol), and three independent correspondence letters in NEJM probe the olezarsen pancreatitis-risk findings reported earlier this year by the TIMI group.

---

**2026 ACC/AHA/AACVPR/ABC/ACPM/ADA/AGS/APhA/ASPC/NLA/PCNA Guideline on the Management of Dyslipidemia**
Blumenthal RS, Morris PB, Gaudino M, Johnson HM, Anderson TS, Bittner VA, et al.
*Circulation*, 28 April 2026
[PMID 41824552](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41824552/) · [DOI: 10.1161/CIR.0000000000001423](https://doi.org/10.1161/CIR.0000000000001423)

The first comprehensive U.S. dyslipidemia guideline since the 2018 cholesterol document. Per the abstract, the new guideline explicitly broadens scope to address evaluation, management, and monitoring of high blood cholesterol, hypertriglyceridemia, and elevated lipoprotein(a) — a structural change that puts SHTG and Lp(a) in the same evidence-graded framework as LDL-C for the first time. The literature base was assembled from a comprehensive search through December 2024, so it predates the most recent olezarsen and plozasiran phase 3 readouts but should incorporate the bulk of the apoC-III / ANGPTL3 / Lp(a)-targeted RNA therapeutic data through that cutoff. Worth reading in full once the body text drops behind the abstract — pay attention to where TG and non-HDL-C thresholds land for primary and secondary prevention, and how (or whether) the document positions APOC3- and ANGPTL3-targeted agents.

---

**Olezarsen for Hypertriglyceridemia and Pancreatitis Risk — three correspondence letters and authors' reply**
*N Engl J Med*, 30 April 2026

NEJM published a cluster of three reader letters and an author reply, all responding to Marston, Bergmark, Sabatine et al., "Olezarsen for Hypertriglyceridemia and Pancreatitis Risk" (*N Engl J Med* 2026;394:429–441; DOI 10.1056/NEJMoa2512761). The letters do not introduce new primary data but are the first published peer commentary on the trial since print release.

- Wang S, Fan Z (Dalian Medical University). [PMID 42054689](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/42054689/) · [DOI: 10.1056/NEJMc2602622](https://doi.org/10.1056/NEJMc2602622)
- Chen R, Lu S, Chen M (Fuzhou University Affiliated Provincial Hospital). [PMID 42054688](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/42054688/) · [DOI: 10.1056/NEJMc2602622](https://doi.org/10.1056/NEJMc2602622)
- Reply: Marston NA, Bergmark BA, Sabatine MS (Brigham and Women's Hospital). [PMID 42054690](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/42054690/) · [DOI: 10.1056/NEJMc2602622](https://doi.org/10.1056/NEJMc2602622)

For context on the underlying paper: olezarsen is a hepatocyte-targeted antisense oligonucleotide against APOC3 mRNA, and the original Marston et al. report described the effect of olezarsen on triglycerides and the rate of acute pancreatitis in patients with severe or persistent hypertriglyceridemia. The full text of the letters and reply is needed to evaluate the specific methodological and clinical points being raised — the abstracts are not informative on their own — but the appearance of three independent letters within three months of print publication is itself a signal that the pancreatitis endpoint and event-rate analysis are getting actively scrutinized by the field.

---

**What to watch.** The next inflection points for severe hypertriglyceridemia are the long-term safety and pancreatitis-event data on the APOC3-directed (olezarsen) and ANGPTL3-directed (zodasiran, evinacumab in HTG populations) agents, plozasiran's progress through its FCS / SHTG phase 3 program, and how the new ACC/AHA guideline frames TG-lowering thresholds and apoC-III / ANGPTL3 therapeutics in clinical decision-making. A genuinely thin week for primary literature in our target space — no RCTs, mechanism papers, or genetics readouts in the top-tier journals.
