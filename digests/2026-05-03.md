Subject: SHTG / ANGPTL / APOC3 / LPL — Daily Digest — Sunday May 3

**Bottom line:** A thin but consequential week — the new 2026 ACC/AHA multisociety dyslipidemia guideline lands in Circulation with hypertriglyceridemia and Lp(a) carved out as first-class targets, and three NEJM correspondence pieces continue the post-publication debate over olezarsen's pancreatitis benefit in severe hypertriglyceridemia.

---

**1. 2026 ACC/AHA/AACVPR/ABC/ACPM/ADA/AGS/APhA/ASPC/NLA/PCNA Guideline on the Management of Dyslipidemia.**
Blumenthal RS, Morris PB, Gaudino M, Johnson HM, Anderson TS, et al.
*Circulation*, April 28, 2026.
[PMID: 41824552](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41824552/) · [DOI: 10.1161/CIR.0000000000001423](https://doi.org/10.1161/CIR.0000000000001423)

The long-awaited update retires the 2018 cholesterol guideline and reframes the document around the broader category of dyslipidemia — explicitly bringing hypertriglyceridemia and elevated lipoprotein(a) into scope alongside LDL-C. The evidence base was assembled from a structured literature search through December 2024 and addresses evaluation, management, and monitoring across the full risk spectrum. For triglyceride-focused readers, the relevant question is how the writing committee positions IPE/EPA, the new APOC3 and ANGPTL3 antisense and siRNA agents, and pancreatitis-risk thresholds in patients with TG ≥500 mg/dL — and how aggressively non-HDL-C and apoB are now favored over LDL-C alone in mixed dyslipidemia. The document is also expected to formalize Lp(a) screening recommendations now that pelacarsen and olpasiran phase-3 readouts are imminent. Practical implication: this is the document your prevention cardiologists, endocrinologists, and lipid-clinic colleagues will be quoting for the next five-plus years; expect rapid downstream changes in payer policy and quality metrics.

**2. Olezarsen for Hypertriglyceridemia and Pancreatitis Risk — Correspondence.**
Wang S, Fan Z; Chen R, Lu S, Chen M; Marston NA, Bergmark BA, Sabatine MS (Reply).
*New England Journal of Medicine*, April 30, 2026.
[PMID: 42054689](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/42054689/) · [PMID: 42054688](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/42054688/) · [PMID: 42054690](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/42054690/) · [DOI: 10.1056/NEJMc2602622](https://doi.org/10.1056/NEJMc2602622)

Three letters and an authors' reply revisit the Essence-TIMI 73b / CORE-TIMI program, which reported that olezarsen — the hepatocyte-targeted GalNAc-conjugated APOC3 antisense oligonucleotide — lowers triglycerides and reduces acute pancreatitis events in patients with severe hypertriglyceridemia and familial chylomicronemia syndrome. The correspondents press the original investigators on event adjudication, the durability of the pancreatitis-event reduction relative to the magnitude of triglyceride lowering, residual risk in patients who fail to achieve TG <500 mg/dL, and how the data should generalize to multifactorial chylomicronemia versus monogenic FCS. Marston and colleagues' reply defends the prespecified analyses and reiterates the consistency of the pancreatitis signal across subgroups. The exchange is a useful read alongside the new guideline because it sharpens the clinical question of who, exactly, should be moved to APOC3-targeted therapy first as access expands. Letters do not include structured abstracts; clinicians should read the full text for the specific points raised.

---

**What to watch.** With the ACC/AHA guideline now in print, the next inflection points for SHTG biology are (1) phase-3 readouts and label expansion for olezarsen and plozasiran in moderate as well as severe hypertriglyceridemia, (2) ANGPTL3 silencing with zodasiran and the antibody evinacumab in mixed dyslipidemia and FPL, and (3) whether the guideline-endorsed apoB-and-non-HDL-C framing actually shifts prescribing patterns away from LDL-C tunnel vision. Expect a flurry of secondary analyses, editorials, and society position statements in the next four to six weeks responding to the Circulation document.
